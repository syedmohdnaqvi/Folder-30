Nouakchott city is the capital and the biggest city in Mauritania. This city tour helps you to get acquainted with the city and know your way around if you’re coming to stay for a period of time. In this tour you will be visiting the national museum which displays archeology, artifacts customs and textiles from prehistory to the present days of Mauritania. Banc d’Arguin national park is a world heritage site. Two million birds make of this ornithological and nature sanctuary one of the three most important migratory birds breeding site on Earth. Oudane

Ouadane is the most remote oasis in Mauritania, built on a rocky hill and founded in 1147. Most of it is in ruins, but some houses are still lived in.

Guelb Richat (The Eye of the Sahara)

Known as the Eye of Africa, Guelb Richat is a circular geological wonder. The largest circle is 40 km (25 miles) around. Scientists think it was once a giant volcano that collapsed and eroded.

Chinguity (The Gateway to the Sahara)

Chinguity was an important trading point between North and Sub-Saharan Africa. Dating back to the 11th century, it has ancient manuscripts and the second oldest mosque in Africa.

Ben Amera

Ben Amera is the second-largest monolith in the world, standing 633 meters (2,027 feet) tall. It is located 4 km north of the train tracks, with a smaller monolith called Aïsha nearby.

Terjit

Terjit is an oasis in a gorge on the western edge of the Adrar plateau. It has a palm grove and a stream from a spring, offering cool water and shade.
